Unit 2.5.2: Polymorphism

Polymorphism means many forms. Here is an example about the concept. We can create a superclass person method, however, a person can be an employee and a student. For example, at college, that person is a student. At McDonalds, that person is an employee. Thus, we use the polymorphism technique to slightly alter the original person method to accordingly fit those different circumstances.

Polymorphism applies to object-oriented programming, and occurs in Java when many classes are related by inheritance. Here is how inheritance & polymorphism are connected:

 - Inheritance inherits attributes and methods from another class.
 - Polymorphism uses those methods and attributes to perform tasks under different situations.
